Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about Cadence—who it’s for, formats, recovery use, and how you get your copy.

Is this book suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. While the insights are backed by clinical sports science and physiotherapy, the content is written to be clear and actionable. Whether you are just starting your fitness journey or you’re a seasoned athlete, you will find practical steps you can implement immediately.

How is this different from a standard fitness magazine?

Most magazines focus on fleeting trends. Cadence is a curated “Health File”—an evidence-informed resource designed to be a permanent part of your library. We skip the “fluff” and provide deep-dive protocols on recovery, nutrition, and biomechanics that stay relevant for years.

Can I get this in a digital format?

Yes! We offer a high-resolution E-book version for those who prefer to have these insights on their tablet or phone for quick reference at the gym. You can also choose the Limited Edition Print version if you prefer a tactile, ad-free reading experience for your home library.

I’m currently injured; can I still use these techniques?

Yes. A significant portion of the book is dedicated to rehabilitation and healing journeys. The guide helps you understand the science of recovery, though we always recommend using it alongside your primary healthcare provider’s advice for your specific condition.

Why should I buy the Print Edition if there is an E-book?

The Print Edition is a limited-run collectible (only 10,000 copies). Many of our readers prefer the physical copy because it allows for “focused learning” without digital distractions, and it serves as a high-quality reference book you can highlight and revisit for years.

How do I receive my E-book after purchase?

Your digital copy will be delivered instantly via email as soon as your order is confirmed. You can download it and start reading on any device immediately.
